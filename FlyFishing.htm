Fly Patterns

Dry Flies: Paterns imitating the adult stage of aquatic insects like mayflies, caddis flies, stonefiles, midges or dragon flies.

* Recommended patterns are Adams, Light Cahills, Elk Hair Caddis, and Yellow Stimulators.

Streamers: Designed to resemble some form of baitfish or other large aquatic prey. Great flies to use when water levels are high.

* Recommended patterns are the Wolly Bugger, and Muddler Minnow

Nymphs: Most trout feeding is below surface wher they forge on Nymphs. Insects drop their eggs on the surface and these drift to the bottom of streams and river where they stay until hatch and the newly developed nymphs are prime food for hungry trout.

* Recommended patterns are Prince Nymphs, Pheasant Tails, Hare's Ear

Leave no Trace

  • Respect the Environment
    • Properly dispose unwanted fishing line or trash
    • Fish entrails and human waste should be buried 6-inches deep in the ground 200 feet from the water
    • Pack it in, pack it out. Pack out everything you bring
  • Respect the Fish
    • Cut barbs off of hooks to limit damage to the fish
    • Keep only the fish you need and return otheres back into the water
    • Limit the time the fish is out of water
  • Respect Others
    • Keep a respectable distance from others recasting on the water
    • Be aware of designated public and private fishing areas
    • Travel and fish quietly, not to disturb the fish of others

Help provide a great fishing experience to the environment, the fish, and others by following Leave No Trace principles
